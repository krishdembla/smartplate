\begin{abstract}
We introduce \textbf{SmartPlate}, a lightweight vision system that turns a single photograph of a plate of food into (i) a semantic segmentation of its components, (ii) a coarse nutritional breakdown, and (iii) a 0--100 \emph{healthy-plate} score grounded in the Harvard Healthy Eating Plate rubric.
Building on a SegFormer-B0 transformer backbone fine-tuned on the FoodSeg103 benchmark, we aggregate the $103$ predicted food classes into $7$ Harvard food groups and convert per-class pixel areas into group-level proportions that drive a simple, interpretable scoring formula.
We compare the segmentation pipeline against a ResNet-50 multi-label classification baseline trained on the same data.
Experiments on the FoodSeg103 validation split report per-class mean IoU, per-food-group mean IoU, and qualitative plate scorings on held-out images.
The system is designed to run end-to-end on consumer hardware (Apple Silicon / PyTorch MPS) and exposes the full pipeline in a reproducible Jupyter notebook.
\end{abstract}
